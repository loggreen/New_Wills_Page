What is a Succession?

A succession is the legal process that you must go through in the courthouse in order to resolve someone's estate after they pass away. Resolving someone's estate means paying off their debts and then distributing their property to their heirs. It is the only way to legally distribute someone's personal property after they pass away.

What are the steps in a typical Succession?

The process of going through a succession can vary depending on many different factors, including whether or not someone passed away with a will, if they had a considerable amount of debt, how many children or grand-children they had, or even how many times they were married. Who is going to inherit all of the estate property?

Who is going to inherit all of the property of an estate depends on whether or not a decedent left behind a valid will and who their surviving family members are. The law also treats someone's individual property and the jointly held property of a marriage differently. It's important to speak with a lawyer to understand what will happen to specific estate property.
