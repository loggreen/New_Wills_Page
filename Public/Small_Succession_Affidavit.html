Small Succession Affidavits

What is a Small Succession Affidavit?

A small succession affidavit is a document that closes an estate. It is typically prepared by a lawyer and signed by most, if not all, of the heirs to someone's estate.

What are the requirements to use a Small Succession Affidavit?

To qualify to use a small succession affidavit, the person who passed away must have had an estate valued at less than $125,000 or have passed away over twenty years ago. They also cannot have had a will. If a decedent fulfills those two requirements, it is very likely that their family members will be able to use a small succession affidavit to close their estate.

What are the benefits of using a Small Succession Affidavit?

Most importantly, using a small succession affidavit is far less costly than the alternatives. This is because the affidavit procedure avoids the need to go to court entirely. That means no filing fees and results in far less work for the lawyer, resulting in a much smaller legal fee. In addition, because you do not need to go to court, using a small succession affidavit is typically a much faster process of resolving someone's estate than going to court.
